Career narrative

How Did Tulika Dutta Become a Nutritionist?

Tulika Dutta's nutrition practice grew from years of one-to-one wellness coaching, then formal Nutrition and Dietetics study, and now a prevention-first practice across Serampore, Khardaha, and online consultations.

  1. 01

    Wellness coaching came first

    Tulika Dutta's work began more than ten years ago with people who wanted to feel better in their own bodies. As a wellness coach, she saw the parts of daily life a diet chart rarely covers: work shifts, family meals, festival weeks, and the frustration of trying again.

  2. 02

    Generic charts kept failing real life

    She kept seeing quick fixes, meal replacements, crash diets, and photocopied calorie charts fall apart when there was no follow-up. The problem was not willpower. The plan had to fit the person's actual routine.

  3. 03

    Formal study added structure

    That experience led Tulika Dutta into formal Nutrition and Dietetics study. The coaching experience came first; professional nutrition training added structure, evidence awareness, and safer decision-making on top of it.

  4. 04

    The practice became prevention-first

    Her approach became personal, practical, and rooted in early course correction: understand food habits, reports, routine, and the reason behind a struggle before handing over a plan.

  5. 05

    Local, online, and accessible

    Today, Tulika Dutta consults from Serampore, Khardaha, and online. Her work stays personal: when someone books with Tulika Dutta, they sit with Tulika Dutta, with Bengali or English support where natural.

Care that follows through

I did not want to be the person who hands you a chart and disappears. I wanted to be the person who is still there when it gets hard.

Philosophy

How Does Tulika Dutta Approach Nutrition?

Tulika Dutta's approach rests on six principles: find the root cause, teach before prescribing, build around familiar food, measure health without shame, follow up every week, and treat prevention as routine.

  1. Root Cause First

    Before writing a plan, Tulika looks at reports, routine, sleep, stress, medicines, food habits, and the reason a struggle keeps returning.

    01
  2. 02

    Teach First, Prescribe Second

    Clients are shown why a change matters, so decisions feel understandable outside the consultation room and not dependent on a printed chart.

  3. Bengali And Local Food Context

    Plans are built around familiar Indian and Bengali meals, local markets, home kitchens, family routines, and food the client can actually repeat.

    03
  4. 04

    Health Without Shame

    Progress is discussed through energy, digestion, reports, measurements, routine, and confidence. Shame is never used as a motivation tool.

  5. Weekly Follow-Up And Adjustment

    The plan is reviewed as work shifts, festivals, travel, health reports, and everyday food decisions change. A plan that cannot bend breaks.

    05
  6. 06

    Prevention And Long-Term Health

    Routine checkups, honest food conversations, and early course correction are treated as normal care, not a last resort.
